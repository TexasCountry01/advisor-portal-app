About Delegate Access
What is a Delegate?

A delegate is a team member who can perform actions on behalf of a member (advisor). This allows office coordinators or support staff to help with case submissions.

Permission Levels Explained
View Only

Delegate can read member's cases and documents but cannot make changes. Useful for supervisors who need to review work.

Submit Cases

Delegate can submit new cases on behalf of the member. This is the most common permission level for office coordinators.

Edit Cases

Delegate can both submit new cases AND edit existing ones. Useful for quality assurance staff who need to update incomplete cases.

Approve Cases

Full administrative access. Can submit, edit, and approve cases. Reserved for senior team members or future admin workflows.

Workshops & Visibility

Delegates should typically be in the same workshop as the member. Members with the same workshop code can see each other in the member list.

Audit Trail

All delegate access grants, modifications, and revocations are logged in the member's audit trail. This ensures full compliance tracking.

Active vs Inactive

You can temporarily disable a delegate by unchecking "Active". This preserves the history without permanently deleting the delegation.
